# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Operators, founders, technical founders, technically forward business owners, and AI platform teams at companies like Anthropic and OpenAI. They are looking for proof that Michael can understand messy business workflows, use AI agents pragmatically, and turn unclear operating problems into purpose-built software that people can actually use.

## Product Purpose

This site presents Michael DeGoll's point of view on purpose-built software, AI agents, local workflows, and the move away from bloated legacy platforms. Success means a visitor quickly understands the work: diagnose the real process, keep or retire existing tools with judgment, build useful software in smaller slices, and use agents to accelerate implementation without creating AI theater.

## Brand Personality

Practical, clear, technically curious, and grounded. The site should feel like it belongs to someone who can operate inside messy real businesses, explain tradeoffs without jargon, and still build quickly. It should be simple, consistent, and pretty, without looking like a generic AI landing page or an over-polished agency deck.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI consultant pages, enterprise SaaS polish, Salesforce-first positioning, corporate blue gradients, vague innovation language, heroic claims about transformation, and AI-demo theater. Avoid anything that makes the site feel like a template for "AI transformation" rather than a specific working point of view about business tools.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the operating problem, not the technology stack.
2. Make the point of view obvious before asking for contact.
3. Prefer direct language over hype, hedging, or consultant filler.
4. Show practical artifacts, migration paths, and implementation judgment.
5. Keep the site lightweight enough that publishing a new idea stays easy.
6. Look credible to operators and technically forward founders while also signaling good taste to Anthropic and OpenAI teams.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, keyboard navigation, responsive behavior, alt text, and focus states. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Do not rely on color alone to communicate meaning.
